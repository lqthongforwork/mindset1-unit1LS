Unit 1: Relationships (Family)

Welcome! In this lesson you will practise IELTS Listening and IELTS Speaking using the topic of family and relationships. Everything is designed for Pre-IELTS learners (band 4.0–5.0), so the language is kept simple and there is lots of help before each task.

🎯 Lesson objectives

By the end of this lesson you will be able to:

  • identify the speakers in a conversation
  • listen carefully for numbers, prices and dates
  • answer IELTS-style multiple-choice, form-completion, matching and short-answer questions
  • talk about your family using useful collocations
  • give full answers in Speaking Part 1, 2 and 3

In Section 1 of the IELTS Listening test you hear two people talking in an everyday situation, for example booking a restaurant or shopping. One person usually needs to find out information from the other.

Before you listen

📖 Pre-listening vocabulary

Learn these words first. Click 🔊 to hear the pronunciation.

Pronunciation focus — numbers that sound similar: be careful with -teen and -ty numbers.

🧠 Listening strategies

1. Predict answers

Read the questions first. Guess what kind of answer you need (a number? a name? a place?).

2. Identify keywords

Underline the important words in each question so you know what to listen for.

3. Listen for numbers, names, dates, places

Details like £318, the 17th, or 20 people are common answers.

4. Recognise paraphrasing

The words you hear are often different from the question. "a leaving party" = "a goodbye party".

5. Avoid distractors

You may hear several numbers, but only one is correct. Listen for words like "actually" or "no, no" that show a correction.

Section 3 · IELTS Speaking
🗣️

Speaking about family

The Speaking test has three parts. Today you will practise all three on the topic of family and relationships. Remember the golden rule for Part 1: give full answers (2–3 sentences) but don't talk too long.

💡 How to extend an answer: add a reason or detail with these words: because, and, but, so, also, although / even though.
Example: "I have one older brother, and we're very close because we like the same games."

🧰 Useful vocabulary & sentence starters

Sentence starters

  • "There are … people in my family: …"
  • "I'm the … (oldest / youngest / middle) child."
  • "We usually … together at the weekend."
  • "I'm most similar to my … because …"
  • "I get on really well with … "
